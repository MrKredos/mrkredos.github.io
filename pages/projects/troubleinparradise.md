## Intro
Trouble in Parradise was a weekly TEKKEN 8 local hosted at Parramatta. 
Hosted at Harry Todd Band Hall, Parramatta, NSW, Australia! This was a weekly tournament that from on 6pm Wednesdays for most weeks of 2025, starting towards the start of the year.

## How did this come about?
Yarou, Kal and frfr were advertising in the OCE Tekken Discord[^discord] about this event, and also crowdsourcing what to call the name of the event. I figured I would put up my hand and volunteer, the worst they can say is no anyways. I've always wanted to branch away from the Western Sydney Fighting Games Club and start working outside for more experience. I figured this was my chance to help out and get more connections, and get my roots deep within the Tekken community. 

I think my intentions were quite selfish, I only wanted to help out so I could improve my skills with the stream stuff, and to get my name out there. This is before I had a full-time job and before I realised what I wanted to do with my life. Opportunities are opportunities, I suppose. 

Reached out to Yarou with my previous experience and just like that I was added to the group chat and the rest is history. 

## Logistics of the Event
How did they get monitors and PS5s for this event? I think this is probably on a lot of people's minds because you don't create such a big event from scratch AND without experience. These guys didn't have anything to begin with and relied pretty heavily on the wider community (not a bad thing, but just means its not sustainable). Danny of Danny's Cafe provided all of the equipment for TiP, which is a Fridays Tekken local in Silverdale and also a cafe/restaurant. Never met the person myself but lending equipment to a couple of friends to run their own event is quite admirable. Danny provided a bunch of PS5s with all the games and DLC, monitors, cables and power extensions. This event wouldn't exist without Danny's help, send them some love!

## Ending of TiP
Not really my story to share I guess, but this is just my best guess. 

I think Yarou was exhausted doing this weekly and there not being enough interest. I'm sure he would have continued otherwise, but you need like 19~ people to break even, and he would barely be getting that towards the end of year due to lack of interest. And I didn't really help the fact, I barely showed up because I'm poor and looking for work (especially towards the tail end of 2025) but I did make an effort to come when I could, even if I didn't play the game. 

Honestly, TEKKEN 8 online is not terrible compared to TEKKEN 7, so weekly locals felt a little more like a bonus than an essential. I remember suggesting we do a ammies or redemption bracket for the more casual members and might garner more interest while also slowly boosting up the lower skill level, but got hit with negative reception from the team to the idea and was shut down on the spot without further discussion. I still believe if they focuesd on the newer players they would have garnered a stronger community and attendance, but you can't expect the world from inexperienced people who also happen to be some of the best players in the area. When you're that good, you kind of only think about yourself and your peers. Experience and hindsight will help you get better perception, so I don't really blame them for having the foresight to see that far ahead. 

Coming from someone who has organised events in the past, I felt there was a lot of passion but not enough experience, and it showed. Too many cooks spoil the broth and nobody taking direct leadership when it felt like there should have been. Things worked out in the end, but I believe at some people's expense. There are a few people I respect more and some who I respect less after this event. I refuse to name drop to keep integrit, I think they all did a great job and we all had a great time. It attracted a few faces I hadn't seen since highschool, made a few new friends and learned a lot more from this experience. 

I think it was inevitable that it would stop at some point, it was only a matter of when. 

### Side Tangent: Addressing the elephant in the room

I do not enjoy being around the online TEKKEN 8 community, I'm mainly talking about the OCE TEKKEN Discord[^discord] (as of July 2026). Oh man. It's just a cess pit. I think this has to be the worst community to actively be a part of, I'm not even joking it will negatively impact your mental health. To put it simply, I think there is a vocal minority that kind of ruins it for everyone else, I don't think they play the game, I don't think they participate in local events, I don't think they give back to community and/or I don't think they actually enjoy the game and may be trying to chase the high given to them from previous TEKKEN entries. Don't get me wrong, there are some lovely people who do exist in this community but a few rotten apples spoil the bunch and you're better off finding people at locals and in smaller circles. The people in this Discord are miserable and will bring you down to their level. This is what you get when you mix of some absolutely degenerate and unhinged individuals who like to limit test on people's patience, with some really insufferable, highly sensitive and/or narcissitic individuals. Two polar opposites colliding playing a miserable game in it's current state, perhaps all the stars have aligned for all of this to take place. And even worse, some of these people who are airing their dirty laundry and causing so much drama and genuinely creating a rift in the community, ARE IN THEIR 30s. Is there really nothing better to do with your time, is this really worth the time and effort?

What happened to "talk is cheap", "run the set" or "settle your beef in game". If you got a problem, RUN THE SET. Where did it all go wrong? 

I hate to end on a bad note. I suppose I still enjoy some people in the TEKKEN 8 community and I have decided to stay clear from certain servers and individuals. I feel like TOs struggle the most because they are caught in the middle of drama that should be considered personal drama and are expected to take a stance. We're all doing this out of the love of the game. 

Anyways... there might be more events soon so stay tuned to the TiP Twitter/X[^x]!
